Chapitre 3
Le déluge
King Lycaon
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Time went by. Humans spread throughout the earth. Generation after generation, their number increased. They constructed the first cities and first kingdoms. But Zeus was cautious of humans. He wanted to make sure they were all good and pious. He disguised himself as a beggar and asked King Lycaon for hospitality.
He could not have found a worse person, because he was a horrible king: he served him a meal of human roasted meat! The King of Gods, rendered mad by rage, turned Lycaon into a wolf and struck with lightning all the inhabitants of the palace who were involved in this crime!
Chez les grecs, le devoir de l’hospitalité est sacré : un voyageur doit être bien accueilli et bien nourri. Il est donc interdit de lui servir ses enfants comme repas !
The fury of Zeus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
But his rage did not abate. He had proof: humans were an evil species. They had to be eradicated from this world! Zeus commanded Aeolus, the God of Winds, to release torrents of rain on earth. Zeus commanded Poseidon, God of the Sea, to cover the earth with water. His waves covered houses, forests, and even the highest mountains.
Soon on earth, there was a sea storm and violent hurricanes. The great flood had begun. It would purge the earth of the dirt and meanness of humans.
The Justs
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Deucalion and Pyrrha were a couple of fishermen. They lived a simple life. They were poor, but they never forgot to perform sacrifices for the gods. Right before Zeus unleashed his wrath, an oracle had told them to get into their boat. They had obeyed the command without objections. When the waters rose above the mountains, they were carried by their boat and did not drown.
Zeus saw them. They were the only survivors of the human race. He had only to lift his finger and his lightning bold would smite them. They were so fragile, so meek, that for once, the king of gods had pity upon them. He decided to spare them. Chained to his rock, Prometheus then knew he had foreseen correctly.
J'ai peur... Si Zeus le veut, ça se passera bien.
The rebirth of men
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
At the end of nine days and nine nights, Deucalion and Pyrrha reached a small island. It was the top of Mount Parnassus that crested above the flood waters. The water withdrew. The oracle told them that to repopulate mankind, they needed to throw the bones of their grandmother over their shoulders. They stood a moment in surprise, and then Pyrrha understood.: the grandmother was the earth, and its bones were stones.
When the water was completely withdrawn, Deucalion and Pyrrha came down the mountain. At each step, they grabbed a rock and threw it over their shoulders. Each time Deucalion would throw one, a man would arise from the earth; each time Pyrrha threw one, it was a woman that grew from the dirt: mankind was reborn!
Bonjour ! Ca va ? Quelle belle journée... Et si on allait par là ?
Toutes et tous nous sommes donc les descendants de Deucalion et Pyrrha, ou du moins des pierres qu’ils ont jeté derrière eux !
