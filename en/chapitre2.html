Chapter 2
The birth of woman
Pandora
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Since Zeus had taken the place of his father, Chronos, no one had dared defy his authority, except for Prometheus. Zeus was still furious and wanted to take vengeance on mankind. He called the rest of the gods and asked them to help him come up with a good solution. It would be Pandora, the first woman. Because of her, , man would never again know peace.
Hephaestus modeled her with clay. Athena taught her the loom; Aphrodite gave her beauty; Apollo taught her how to sing and, finally, Hermes taught her cunning and curiosity. She was a marvelous and irresistible creature. Zeus would use her for the detriment of mankind.

The first wedding
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Epimetheus was the brother of Prometheus. He was also the father of man, and lived with them and among them. He was not very shrewd, but he had a simple and just heart.
Zeus gave Pandora in marriage to Epimetheus. He gave the couple a closed jar, forbidding them to open it. Epimetheus was charmed by the beauty of his new wife, and wrapped her in loving care. He was very much in love and he worked hard to give her the best. Every day, he left at dawn to cultivate his field and take his cows to graze. During this time, Pandora worked on the loom, cleaned the house and prepared meals. The two lovers were happy.
I love you Epimetheus. I love you Pandora.
Curiosity is a bad trait
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Unfortunately, the curiosity that Hermes had lodged in Pandora’s heart was torturing her: night and day, she dreamed of opening the forbidden jar. What could be in there?
One day, when Epimetheus was gone, she could no longer resist and opened the lid. Zeus had enclosed within the jar all the woes of man. They escaped, howling as they went: old age, sickness, war, famine, madness, deception, theft… These were terrible birds. They took to the air and have accompanied mankind ever since. Pandora, frightened, sought to close the jar. But the last bird escaped: he was called “Hope”.

Hope
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Men and women were now united together for the same fate. They would confront every danger. They would know woes, suffering, sickness, and old age. They would end up by dying. But before that, they would have children, who in their turn would grow up, suffer, and make more children. Hope would guide their whole life. It was the engine that allowed them to progress and to get up each morning. Hope gave them the desire to invent, explore the world , or write songs. Humans were not animals like the others. They would never cease advancing, because they wanted to create a better world.
