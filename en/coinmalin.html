The prehistoric men

Today, we know that human beings were not created by gods. They have evolved and descend from species of monkeys who have since disappeared. And to arrive at the humans we think of nowadays, there were many interim steps.

Lucy

Lucy is 3.2 million years old. It is the name given to a skeleton found in 1974 and which has long been considered been the most ancient known human. She belongs to the species of Australopithecus. She was approximately 25 years old and measured 1m10 and 1m20. Source Wikipedia: Reconstitution of Lucy in the National Museum of Nature and Sciences of Tokyo.

D.R.

Homo Habilis

Homo Habilis lived between 2.5 and 1.5 million years ago. We attribute the first tools to him.

D.R.

Homo Erectus

He lived between 1.5 million years ago to roughly 100,000 years ago. He owes his name due to the fact he stood upright on his two feet.

D.R.

Neanderthal

He lived between 250,000 and 28,000 years ago. Less intelligent than Homo Sapiens, he was not able to adapt to climactic changes and eventually disappeared.

D.R.

Cro Magnon

The Cro-Magnon is the cave man. We descend directly from him

D.R.

Homo Sapiens

It is the Human being as we know it. It is your mom and dad, and it is you.

D.R.

Agriculture

Our history tells us how men learned agriculture which consists in growing vegetable for food.

Growing cereals

Cereals are plants that are grown exclusively for their grains. Examples are: wheat, corn, rice... Cereals today represent 45% of the calories eaten by humans.

©Wikipedia

Gardens

In a garden, we grow plants utilized for food. These are legumes. For example: cabbage, potatoes, zucchinis, etc.

D.R.

Growing Fruit Trees

In a plant, a flower turns into fruits. Many fruits, but not all, grow on trees. They are often sweet and eaten for desert. For example apples, peach apricots.

D.R.

Traditional Agriculture

In many regions of the world, agriculture has not evolved in centuries. It suffers often from a lower yield than industrial agriculture but it is more respectful of the environment.

Agriculture in Madagascar

Intensive agriculture

Intensive agriculture seeks to greatly improve the profitability and produce more with less workers. It allows us to nourish a human population that is ever growing but it is otherwise very bad for the environment. It utilizes many polluting machines, chemical fertilizers and pesticides,

D.R

Biological Agriculture

Biological agriculture does not use chemical fertilizers or biologically modified organisms. It is better for one’s health and is better for the environment than intensive agriculture. But it costs more and the consumers must pay more.

Vegetables grown from Biological Agriculture

Urban Agriculture

In developed countries, many people lives in cities and can’t have a garden. More and more people developed a new form of agriculture developed for the urban environment.

Grapevine growing vertically on the façade of a house

Who's who ?

Agenor

Agenor is the king of Sidon. He is the son of the god Poseidon and a mortal named Lybia who is in turn, the daughter of the river Nile.

Aphrodite

Aphrodite is the goddess of beauty and love. She is born when Chronos cut a piece of flesh of Ouranos (Uranus). Her story is told in our book “the Origin of the World.”

D.R.

Apollon

Appolon is the god of art, singing and poetry. He is the son of Zeus the goddess Leto.

D.R.

Athena

Athena is the goddess of wisdom. She is the daughter of Zeus and the goddess Metis.

D.R.

Bacchants

The Bacchants are the servants of the god Dionysus. They are often irritated and it is best not to aggravate them.

D.R.

Calliope

Calliope is the goddess of epic poetry. She is one of the nine muses, the goddesses of Art. She has 8 sisters and those are the daughters of Zeus and Mnemosyne, the goddess of memory.

D.R.

Chiron

Chiron is a centaur, a creature half man and half horse. He has brought up many heroes and even some gods.

D.R.

Chronos

Chronos is the god of Time, the son of Ouranos, god of the Sky and Gaia, goddess of the Earth. He was king of the world before Zeus chased him away. You can read his story in our book: “the Origin of the World”.

D.R.

Aeolus

Aeolus is the god of the winds. Some say he is the son of Poseidon the god of the seas.

D.R.

Hades

Hades is the brother of Zeus and Poseidon. It’s the god of hell, the underground world where people go after their death.

D.R.

Héphaistos

Hephaistos is the god of artisans and smiths. He is the son of Zeus and his wife Hera.

D.R.

Hera

Hera is a goddess and the wife of Zeus, king of gods.

D.R.

Hermes

Hermes is the god of storekeepers and thieves. He is the son of Zeus and a goddess called Maia.

D.R.

Poseidon

Poseidon is the god of the sea and the brother of Zeus.

D.R.

Zeus

He is the king of world and the son of Chronos. He is married with the goddess Hera. But he often falls in love with other women, whether mortal or goddesses and he has many, many children.

D.R.
