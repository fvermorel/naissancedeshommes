The beginnings of humanity
DFragile creatures

Amidst lions, gazelles, fish and birds, men were not very impressive, having neither claw nor tooth to defend themselves or attack, nor fur to protect themselves from the cold. They were incapable of running fast or flying in the air to avoid danger. They could not swim like fish, nor burrow like rabbits. Men went forward naked, shivering in the cold. They had to dig in the earth to eat roots. Lions, wolves and any other fearsome animal could devour them. How could such a meek, defenseless creature survive?
The fire of Olympus
 ⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The titan Prometheus took pity upon mankind. Having fought against the tyranny of Chronos, he was fair, and felt the injustice of men’s condition: they needed something! Discretely, Prometheus grabbed the fire of Olympus and hid it inside a hollow reed.
With fire, the life of humans would not be the same: they could heat and defend themselves against creatures , and cook food. They could make weapons of steel and become masters of the earth. They would no longer be the most miserable of the animals. Prometheus had turned them into the most fearsome.
Prométhée est un titan, fils de Japet qui est lui même fils d’Ouranos, le ciel. Ca en fait le cousin de Zeus, comme vous pourrez l’apprendre dans notre livre “L’origine du monde”.
The sharing of the bull
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
When Zeus, the King of Gods, realized that Prometheus had given fire to man, he became infuriated. The titan had acted without asking his permission! Zeus decided to exact revenge upon mankind. He condemned them to give half of all their wealth and food to him.
Prometheus once again went to help the humans. When they sacrificed the first bull, he told them to divide it in two: one part that seemed bigger and looked good, but that only contained bones, and the other part that seemed smaller, but where there was meat and fat. Zeus fell into the trap and took the bony part. The rules of the sharing were therefore fixed: humans could enjoy the results of their labor in peace.

Prometheus gets punished
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
It was really too much for Zeus, who began to hate Prometheus. He asked Hermes and Ares, his two sons, to stop the titan. Then, he chained Prometheus to a rock to condemn him to a horrible torture: each day, an eagle would come from the sky and devour his liver. Each day, his liver would grow back, and the next day, the cycle would start over again.
Without Prometheus, mankind would have had a miserable existence, worse than worms. He had paid dearly for his love for them, but having kept the gift of premonition, he knew that, one day, someone would free him: the son of Zeus and a mortal.
Ce fils de Zeus, qui délivrera Prométhée, c’est Héraklès, le plus grand des héros grec, celui qui accomplira les fameux douze travaux.
