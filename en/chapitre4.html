The first kingdoms
The rapture of Europa
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Once again, men and women were multiplying on the surface of the earth. They regrouped and organized with one another: they once again began building great cities. They once again chose kings to govern over them.
One day, next to the town of Sidon, the daughter of King Agenor, Europa, was out for a stroll. Zeus was passing nearby. He found her quite pretty and wanted to take her as a mistress. But he did not want Hera, his wife, to find out and make a scene. So he transformed himself into a white bull and went to graze right next to her feet. The princess, amused, climbed on the back of the animal. At once, the bull took off , running so fast that he began to fly above the waves. The servants of Europa yelled loudly.
Tu as bien compris : si, dans un champ, tu croises un taureau, surtout n’essaie pas de monter dessus. Tu ne sais pas ce qu’il pourrait t’arriver !
Crete
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Zeus and Europa arrived at the island of Crete. No one had followed them and Hera was not aware of anything. They hid and lived together for many years. Thanks to Europa, Zeus was forever reconciled with the human species: never again would he try to exterminate them.
Zeus and Europa had a child they called Minos. Minos became the first king of Crete, the most powerful one of his time. He built an enormous fleet and became the master of the Mediterranean. His story is told in our book Minos and Theseus.
Quel beau bébé ! Ahreu Je vais en faire beaucoup d'autres...
The quest of Cadmos
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Agenor, the father of Europa, was inconsolable. He charged his son Cadmos to go find his daughter. The prince went on the quest, accompanied by his courage and his companions.
Alas! How could he find, in this vast world, a princess who had been kidnapped by a god? He searched for years, in vain. One day, the oracle told Cadmos to abandon his quest: Europa was happy where she was, and he should let her be. In addition, it would be wise not to annoy Zeus!
The gods gave him another mission: he had to start a new city, at a location to be shown to him by a magnificent white cow that was going into the woods…
The founding of Thebes
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Cadmos followed her for days and days. Finally, the animal stopped and slept in the middle of a shaded grove: it was certainly an ideal spot to found a new city. Unfortunately, the region was devastated by a horrible dragon: he had to get rid of him at all costs.
Cadmos attacked the dragon during his sleep. He killed him with a single stroke of his sword. Then, he pulled out all his teeth and planted them as if they were seeds of wheat. Warriors with weapons instantaneously rose from the earth and rushed to kill one other, leaving only five survivors. They made peace and chose Cadmos as their king. Together they founded the city of Thebes, one of the most fabulous of the Greek cities.
Hourra ! Victoire ! Vive Cadmos !
Thèbes sera une ville qui abritera beaucoup de héros très célèbres. Le plus fameux d’entre eux, Oedipe, aura un destin tragique que nous vous raconterons dans un prochain livre.
The founding of Troy
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Where did Dardanus come from? No one knew for sure. Some thought he was the first to come out from the earth when Deucalion and Pyrrha had thrown the stones. Some said he had lived before the Flood, and that he was so strong that for days he had swum in the middle of a stormy sea without drowning. When the water receded, he had founded a very famous city: Troy.
Dardanus had one son, Ilus, who himself had a son, Laomedon, who then became the king of Troy. To protect his town from invaders, Laomedon asked two gods, Apollo and Poseidon, to build him great walls. Alas! Laomedon was a miser. He did not pay his tribute to the two gods. His decision would bring himself, his city , and its residents great disasters. They can be read about in our book Hercules.
The founding of Athens
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Cecrops was a strange creature, half man, half serpent. He wandered throughout Greece. One day, he went to Attica. The land was beautiful, the water was pure and the sky was blue. He decided to stay and found a city.
He was just and wise. So, despite his strange appearance, the residents chose him as king. He taught mankind many things. He taught them writing, which allowed them to remember things. He also taught his subjects to worship Zeus: if they did this, they would stay in his good graces. Never again would the wrath of the gods fall upon them.
Quelle jolie robe ! Tout ceci est fort intéressant. Je ne comprends pas bien.
Athènes est à présent la capitale de la Grèce. Pendant l’antiquité classique, c’est la ville qui a donné naissance au premier des philosophes : Socrate.
